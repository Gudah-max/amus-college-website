A Record of Excellence

Year After Year

Amus College School has built one of Uganda's most consistent academic records — recognised nationally and celebrated by students, teachers, and families alike.

In 2024, three students achieved the perfect score of 20 out of 20 points, and 44 students earned admission to public universities on national merit — a remarkable achievement that reflects the dedication of both our students and teachers.

In 2025, we raised the bar even further. Eighteen students scored 20 out of 20, and 43 students secured government-sponsored university admissions on national merit. These results confirm our position as one of Uganda's leading academic institutions.

In 2023, Amus College School was ranked 3rd in Uganda, a testament to the commitment and passion of our students, teachers, and support staff. That year, 146 Senior Four candidates and 200 Senior Six candidates sat their UNEB examinations successfully.

Arts & Performance

Music, Dance & Drama

Dominating the stage since 2017 — at Teso Zone, nationally, and now internationally.

Amus College School has been a force to be reckoned with in Music, Dance, and Drama (MDD) competitions. Since 2017, we have won the Teso Secondary Schools MDD Competition every single year — a streak of dominance that continues to this day.

At the national level, we have achieved 3rd place nationally for three consecutive years: in 2023 (held in Arua), 2024 (held in Jinja), and 2025 (held in Wakiso, Buddo). Our performers bring passion, creativity, and cultural pride to every stage they step on.

🎵 Chapel Choir Goes International

In 2025, the ACS Chapel Choir finished 3rd at the International Hymn Festival, hosted at the prestigious ACK Mombasa Memorial Cathedral in Kenya — bringing international recognition to our school's musical talent.

Leadership & Service

Scouts: Leading with Purpose

Our scouts develop leadership, entrepreneurship, and environmental stewardship — on the national stage and on school grounds.

The Amus College School Scout Club has earned a reputation for excellence that extends far beyond the classroom. Our scouts have developed invaluable skills in leadership, entrepreneurship, patriotism, and environmental conservation — embodying the spirit of service that defines a true Amus student.

Through hands-on initiatives such as tree planting, our scouts have established a thriving mango farm right here on school grounds — a living example of sustainability in action.

Our scouts have represented the district at the national level from 2023 through to 2024, and in 2024 they won the prestigious Sustainability Award at the Kaazi National Scout Camping and Training Ground — one of Uganda's most competitive scouting events.
