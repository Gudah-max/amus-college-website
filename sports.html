East African Champions — FEASSA 2024

In 2024, Amus College School was crowned East African Champions in Boys' Football at the FEASSA (Federation of East African Secondary Schools Sports Associations) Games — an event we had the honour of hosting right here on our grounds. We defeated St. Mary's Kitende 1-0 in a memorable final, showcasing exceptional talent and teamwork. In the same year, our boys reached another national final, narrowly losing to St. Julian in Masaka.

On the International Stage

Competing at the World Level

Our boys' team represented Uganda at the International Schools Championship in China (2024), where they drew 2–2 with the host nation in normal time before losing on penalties — a courageous performance on the world stage. Our girls' team also represented Uganda, reaching the semi-finals of the same competition.

National Women's Champions

Girls' Football Glory

In 2023, our girls' football team won the National Trophy in Mbarara, defeating St. Noa Girls 1–0. The winning goal was scored by Shalyne Opisa — a moment the entire school community will never forget.

Amus College School also fields a team in the Finance Trust Bank FUFA Women Super League. In 2025, the team won the Uganda Cup with a 1–0 victory over Kawempe Muslim at Philip MTN Omondi Stadium — bringing national glory home to Bukedea.

Teso Zone — Three in a Row

Amus College School has dominated the Teso Zonal Games since the competition's inception in 2014. We have won the Teso Zonal Championship for both boys and girls for three consecutive years — 2024, 2025, and 2026.

Championship Titles

East African & National Champions

Our boys' basketball team has established itself as the finest in the region. In 2024, Amus College School won the FEASSA East African Basketball Championship, hosted right here at ACS and Bukedea Comprehensive School — a proud moment for our entire community.

We did not stop there. In 2025, the team returned to win the National Basketball Championship, hosted at Bukedea Comprehensive School, cementing our status as the dominant force in Ugandan school basketball. These victories are a product of years of training, discipline, and an unrelenting will to win.
